Konnect

 Have you ever moved to a new place? How easy was it for you to meet people and make friends? Challenging right?

Friendship needs time to grow. But as we grow up, it is more and more difficult to find the time and energy to develop close friendships. Taking care of our family, managing our career goals, spending time with our partner... We have busy lifes.

I've been living in Amsterdam for 2 years now (and loving it so far!), but developing close friendships here is challenging for me (COVID doesn't help). So I decided to deep dive into this assumption and see if other people felt the same.

User research: 10 things I learned about friendship

I made 2 surveys, conducted 4 users interviews, gathered qualitative data from 15 people in an open questions form, and spend hours scrolling through the internet. See below what I've learned. Curious to see more? Check my affinity diagram.

Problem statement

Adults who moved to a new place less than 5 years ago

Need to meet people who share their values and interests and are willing to develop a friendship

So that they have people with whom to talk to and share memories in their current area

Ideate

My classmates helped me to generate ideas with a  Crazy 8 workshop.

I continue to brainstorm by generating some  how might we statement, and some  worst ideas.

It helped me to define what will be my concept:

Konnect is an application where you can organize activities, and find people who are willing to join you. You choose the activity, the date, the time, and the number of participants (maximum 6).

This concept was tested with success on users thanks to a survey. 83,1% of the participants said they would use this application.

Design positivity

According to my concept testing survey,  50% of the users wanted to be able to select the participants who will join an activity they organized.

But I also learned during the research phase that some of Tinder's users felt ever more lonely after using it than before.

By providing the option to people to accept or rejects participants, I would gave them the opportunity to select exactly the people they want to meet. At the same time, I'll will contribute to a negative feeling of rejection, that already exists on existing social application.

 Based on this observation I decided to remove the approval/rejection of participants from my list of features.

Design against systemic racism

The second challenge I had to face came up after a discussion I had with an interviewee.

He was explaining to me that he felt discriminated in the past on some social networks because of his skin color: he was not able to find drivers on a famous French carpooling application. After switching his profile picture to a white man, he was able to find drivers way easily. And it triggered my reflexion even further.

You don't make friends with people because of their skin color, or because of their profile picture. You make friends with people because they share values or interests with you.

This assumption was validated by my survey results, so I decided to  remove any information that could trigger systemic discrimination: name, profile picture, nationality, occupation etc.

Test, test and retest

Of course I started with a paper prototype that I've tested on 2 people.

Then I jumped into a mid-fi prototype that I tested in Maze..

66 people tested the Mid-fi prototype

It helped me to identify the critical elements to display on the screen, and to make a list of what was missing.

For example, since there is no profile picture, I had to add a way for people to recognize each other (eg: I will wear a red scarf).
